Flatland — Future Ideas
========================
Inspired by Carl Sagan's Cosmos and Edwin Abbott's Flatland

Deepen the Core Lesson
----------------------

Live Geometry Readout

  Show what Flatlanders actually measure: number of sides, area, perimeter,
  "appeared from nowhere" events. For a sphere: r = sqrt(R^2 - h^2). That
  turns it from a demo into a math lab.


Make It More Interactive
------------------------

Scrubbable Timeline
  Record a pass-through and scrub back and forth. Great for classrooms:
  freeze the moment the apparition is a point, a circle, a square, etc.

Inhabitant Perspectives
  Tap a square or triangle and get a popup in their voice:
  "I saw a thing appear, grow, and vanish. It could not have been real."
  That brings in Abbott's original story, not just Sagan's physics demo.

"Explain This" Overlays
  Optional labels in the 3D view: vertex, edge, face, slice plane, hidden
  dimension. Toggle for beginners vs. advanced users.


Story and Atmosphere
--------------------

Voice Narration
  A calm Cosmos-style voiceover synced to the demo would make it feel much
  more like Sagan's original segment.

Presentation Mode
  Fullscreen, large text, auto-advancing demo, no controls — basically a
  museum/kiosk version.


Visual Upgrades
---------------

Ghost Projection
  Show the full 3D object faintly below/above the plane so users see both
  the solid and the slice at once.

Comparison Inset
  Small inset: "3D truth" vs. "2D perception" with matching highlights.

Custom Slice Styles
  Wireframe apparition, heatmap by "time visible," or pulsing edges when
  the shape first pierces the plane.


Platform-Specific (especially for the Swift app)
------------------------------------------------

visionOS / iOS
  Put Flatland on a real table in AR: shape descends through a physical plane
  on the desk. Very memorable.

Mac Polish
  Keyboard shortcuts (Space = play demo, arrows = height), menu bar, Touch Bar
  height scrubber, export screenshot/video.

Accessibility
  VoiceOver descriptions like: "A circle of radius 0.8 has appeared at the
  center of Flatland."


Stretch Ideas
-------------

  - Puzzle mode: "What 3D shape produced this 2D shadow?"
  - User-drawn 2D shapes → app guesses possible 3D sources
  - Hypercube net unfolding animation
  - Classroom worksheet export (PDF with questions)
  - Cosmos chapter card with timestamp/reference to the original episode


Top Three High-Impact Additions
-------------------------------

  1. 4D hypercube mode — best intellectual payoff
  2. Guided chapters + scrubbable timeline — best for teaching
  3. Inhabitant tap-to-react — best for engagement/story
